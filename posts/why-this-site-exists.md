I rebuilt this site because the old version of it wasn't saying anything my resume didn't already say. So here's an actual post, on an actual blog, about what this thing is for.

## What I'll write about

Three things, roughly:

- **AI tools I'm actually using.** Not hype, not "AI will change everything" takes — just what a given model or feature is actually good at, where it falls apart, and whether it changed how I work that week.
- **Project write-ups.** The projects page has the summary version. Here I'll get into the part that doesn't fit in a bullet point: why the Bexar County map used a KD-tree instead of a naive distance loop, what broke in the OpenAlex pipeline, that kind of thing.
- **The messy parts.** Cleaning 1.2 million records is not a highlight reel. Most of the actual work in data science is figuring out why two datasets disagree about how many people live in a census block group. I'd rather write about that honestly than pretend every project went in a straight line.

## Where I'm coming from

I'm a junior at Trinity studying Business Analytics and Data Science, which means I'm learning this field at the same time the field is being rewritten by AI. I don't think that's a bad time to be learning it — it just means I'm going to be specific about what I've actually built versus what I'm working toward, instead of letting the two blur together.
